## The Problem (Video — 0:30-1:15)

### What is MAP?

MAP (Measures of Academic Progress) by NWEA is one of the most widely used adaptive assessments in K-12 education. Over 13 million students take it across 140+ countries. It's computer-adaptive — questions get harder after correct answers and easier after incorrect ones, zeroing in on the difficulty level where a student answers correctly about 50% of the time. That point becomes their RIT score (Rasch Unit, scale of 100-350), a grade-independent measure of what a student knows and is ready to learn next.

### The Pattern Nobody Talks About

Here's what happens to advanced students:

1. **Fall:** Student takes MAP. Scores high — 90th+ percentile. They're ahead.
2. **The teacher sees the data.** 5 kids are below grade level. 10 are on track. 3 are ahead.
3. **The teacher does the right thing** — focuses instruction on the kids who need to catch up.
4. **The advanced student waits.** Same material. No new challenges. No next-level concepts.
5. **Winter:** MAP test again. The test is adaptive — it asks questions above grade level. The student hasn't been taught those concepts. Score plateaus. Peers catch up. Percentile drops.
6. **Next year:** Reset. The cycle repeats.

### The Real Data

This isn't hypothetical. Here's an actual student's journey:

| Year | Term | Percentile | What Happened |
|------|------|-----------|---------------|
| Kindergarten | Fall | 98-99th | Started strong |
| Kindergarten | Spring | 73-81st | Dropped — no enrichment |
| 1st Grade | Fall | 90-94th | Reset — ahead again |
| 1st Grade | Winter | 83-89th | Dropping again |
| 2nd Grade | Fall | 86-90th | Pattern continues |
| 2nd Grade | Winter | 77-83rd | Still dropping |

The student's raw ability keeps growing. But the gap between what they *could* achieve and what they *do* achieve widens every year. This is called the **excellence gap** — and it affects millions of students.

### Why Teachers Can't Fix This Alone

This isn't a teacher problem. It's a system problem:

- Teachers have 25+ students with different needs
- MAP provides a Learning Continuum with a "Reinforce / Develop / Introduce" framework — showing skills in 10-RIT bands that a student should reinforce (mastered), develop (working on), or be introduced to (ready for next)
- But translating that into actual lessons and exercises for individual students takes hours teachers don't have
- There's no automated tool that reads a student's RIT score and generates targeted "Introduce" band activities

## The Solution (Video — 1:15-2:15)

### Introducing MAP Accelerator

MAP Accelerator is a Gemma 4-powered learning agent that bridges the gap between what MAP *measures* and what students *need*.

**For the student:** A personal tutor that knows exactly what they're ready to learn next — not what the class is learning, but what THEY should be learning.

**For the teacher/parent:** Actionable insights and ready-made enrichment activities that would take hours to create manually.

### How It Works — The "Wow" Moment

**Step 1: Upload MAP Scores**
Parent or teacher photographs the MAP scorecard (or enters RIT scores manually). Gemma 4's multimodal vision reads the document directly.

**Step 2: Instant Analysis**
The agent maps RIT scores to NWEA's Learning Continuum using the Reinforce/Develop/Introduce framework:

- **Reinforce** (RIT band below score) — skills the student has mastered
- **Develop** (RIT band at score) — skills the student is working on now
- **Introduce** (RIT band above score) — skills the student is ready for but hasn't been taught

*"Your child scored RIT 196 in Math (2nd Grade, 83rd percentile). Their 'Introduce' band (RIT 201-210) includes: early multiplication concepts, introduction to fractions, and measurement with standard units. These are the skills to focus on — not what the class is doing."*

**Step 3: Personalized Learning Path**
The agent generates a targeted learning plan with:

- Exercises at the RIGHT difficulty level (not too easy, not too hard)
- Visual and interactive problems (leveraging Gemma 4's multimodal output)
- A suggested weekly schedule parents can follow at home

**Step 4: Adaptive Practice**
The student works through exercises. The agent:

- Explains concepts step-by-step (extended thinking mode)
- Adjusts difficulty based on responses
- Tracks mastery of each skill
- Celebrates progress

**Step 5: Progress Report**
After a session, the agent generates a report for the teacher or parent:

- What was practiced, what was mastered, what needs more work
- Recommended next steps
- Predicted impact on next MAP score
